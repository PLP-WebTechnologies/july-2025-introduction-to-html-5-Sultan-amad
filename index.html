Our Planets

The Inner Planets

The inner planets, also known as terrestrial planets, include Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars. These planets are rocky and have solid surfaces.

  • Mercury: The closest planet to the Sun, known for its extreme temperature variations.

  • Earth: Our home, the only known planet with life.
  • Mars: The red planet, famous for its potential to support life.

The Outer Planets

The outer planets, or gas giants, include Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. These planets are massive and primarily composed of gases.

  • Jupiter: The largest planet, known for its Great Red Spot.
  • Saturn: Famous for its stunning ring system.
  • Uranus: A tilted planet with a faint ring system.
  • Neptune: Known for its deep blue color and strong winds.

Stars and the Universe

Stars are massive celestial bodies that produce light and heat through nuclear fusion. Our Sun is a medium-sized star, essential for life on Earth.

Explore constellations, galaxies, and the mysteries of the cosmos through ongoing astronomical research.

Space Exploration

Humanity has ventured into space with missions like Apollo, Voyager, and the International Space Station. Modern exploration includes rovers on Mars and telescopes like the James Webb Space Telescope.
